Decision making process helper

Important notes

Quorum = minimum number of people needed to be present

Review time = e.g. 12 months before review, unless e.g. 50% vote to revisit decision/policy in a meeting of e.g. 75% or more of the employees

Standard decision criteria = affordable, fair/equitable, legal, attractive, simple.

Test for concensus = Voting options are: YES | NO (voice your concern) | LEGITIMATE BLOCK (no way!) with proposal for amendment | STANDING ASIDE (abstain = consent to go with group decision).

Meeting ground rules

  • name what needs to be named (don't skirt the issue, express how people are feeling)
  • assume positive intent
  • consciously learn
  • confidentiality contract
  • agree and consider time for finish of meeting
  • acknowledge and use the group memory
  • be clear on 'must' vs 'want'
  • allow fishbowl discussions
  • have breakout groups where necessary to answer questions and complete tasks
  • stack participants (facilitator to remember speaking order*)
  • ensure there are sufficient silences and pauses between speakers (use a conch to allow nervous people speaking time!)
  • take sufficient breaks
  • evaluate the meeting

*it helps if the faciliatator is not the person making the proposal or amendment and is more neutral

Decision making process helper

Quora

Agree the percentage of the company required to vote on the issue.

Then agree, of the voting people, what percentage of 'yes' votes would be needed to pass amendments/the proposal.

I.e. someone may say we only need half the company to vote at all, and 55% yes votes would win. Someone else may say we need 75% of the company to vote, and 75% yes votes to pass. It will depend how strongly people feel about the issue and associated risks/benefits. If necessary, take a democratic majority wins vote on what quorate should be.

If it was a draw, ask the people who voted 'no' to suggest amendments or alternative proposals which would allow them to vote 'yes'.

Consider the time people may need to draft their amendments, schedule another meeting to repeat the vote on their amendments.

In the meeting:

Re-vote on each amendment to be included in the proposal.

Repeat this process until it is no longer a draw.

Some people may wish to propose a significantly different proposal - a whole new approach rather than an amendment. An example may be a suggestion to keep things as they are. This should be treated as an alternative proposal and voted on alongside the original proposal, where people can only vote 'yes' on one or the other.
